Section 1: Explanation
What Prompt Engineering is
Prompt Engineering is the skill of writing clear, specific instructions so an AI tool gives you the answer you actually want — instead of a vague, generic one. Think of it like giving directions: "go somewhere nice" gets you nowhere useful, but "drive to the Italian restaurant on 5th Street, table for two at 7pm" gets exact results.
Why it matters when using AI tools like Claude
Claude can't read your mind. It responds to the words you give it — so vague input naturally leads to vague output. The quality of your prompt directly controls the quality, accuracy, and usefulness of the response. Two people can ask "the same question" and get completely different results just because of how they phrased it.
Three practical benefits of writing better prompts

Faster results — you skip multiple rounds of "no, that's not what I meant"
More accurate, specific answers — tailored to your exact situation instead of generic advice
More control over tone, format, and depth — you decide if the answer is short, detailed, technical, or casual


Section 2: Weak vs. Improved Prompt Example
❌ Weak Prompt

"Write about marketing."

Result: A generic, unfocused paragraph that could apply to any business, any audience, any goal.
✅ Improved Prompt

"Act as a digital marketing strategist. Write a 3-step Instagram content plan for a small bakery trying to attract local customers in their first month. Keep it practical and beginner-friendly."

Result: Specific, actionable steps tailored to a real business, a real platform, and a real goal.
What changed: the improved prompt adds a role, a specific goal, a target audience, and a format — turning a vague request into a targeted one.
